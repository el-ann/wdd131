Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How can I create and maintain protective styles for my natural hair?

To create protective styles like braids, twists, or cornrows, start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a leave-in conditioner and shea butter to lock in moisture. When wearing the style, avoid making it too tight to prevent damage. Maintain it by moisturizing regularly with natural oils like coconut or palm kernel oil, wearing a satin scarf or bonnet at night, and keeping your scalp clean.

What are the best products for keeping my braids looking fresh and healthy?

To keep your braids looking fresh and healthy, use:

Moisturizing Sprays: Keeps your scalp and braids hydrated, like As I Am JBCO Water.
Dry Shampoo: Cleans your scalp without disturbing the braids, available at most beauty supply stores.
Light Oils (like coconut or palm kernel oil): Adds shine and prevents dryness.
Edge Control Gel: Keeps your hairline neat and smooth, such as Eco Styler Gel.

What are the best styling techniques for braids and wigs that work with my natural hair type?

For braids, try different braid patterns like box braids, cornrows, or Ghana braids. Use accessories like beads or cowrie shells for added flair. For wigs, ensure a proper fit and secure it with wig caps or adhesive if needed. Style the wig with heat tools or rollers according to your natural hair type, and maintain it with proper washing and conditioning.
